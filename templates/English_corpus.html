You are welcome to the English Audio Recording Page

This recorder records wav/PCM audio (48 kHz sampling rate) directly in the browser. It uses Matt Diamond‘s Recorder.js recorder, which is a popular JavaScript library for recording audio in the browser as uncompressed pcm audio in .wav containers.

The purpose of this page is to collect accented English corpus for the transcripts (available in the Transcript Page ) which are replica of the Yoruba corpus collected in Yoruba Corpus Page

To fully utilise this recording and get the expected recording, kindly take note of the following instructions:

1. Select the range of sentences allocated to you from the transcript page.
2. Ensure your accent is properly taking care of in the recording.
3. Ensure you are cautious of the pace at which you pronounce each word in the sentence. Do not be too fast and do not be too slow.
4. Ensure your recorded words match that provided in the transcript index chosen. If you mistakenly deviate, kindly reload the page to start over.
5. Full pronunciation of all punctuation marks should be carried out e.g. you say "comma", "full-stop", "hyphen" etc. You also pronounce bracket as "open baracket" or "close bracket"
6. Exception to rule 5 is to pronounce words e.g. kg/m3 as "kilogram per meter cube" and not "kilogram slash meter cube."
7. Abbreviations should be pronounced in full. e.g. mmHg should be pronounced as "millimeters of mercury", For the clinical domain data, words using the most known pronunciations by people. e.g. CT should be pronounced as "see tee" and not as "Computed Tomography", "CXR" should be pronounced "Chest Xray" and not "see ex arr"

8. For saving of audio file, if a text file has tag "TEDTALK_PARTA_3_", after recording, to save such file, use the tag name_index no"_ENG"
9. For the instance, if a text file is picked inside a file document "TED_TALK_MALARIA_ENGLISH_TRANSCRIPT_PARTA_3" whose tag is "TEDTALK_PARTA_3_", and at index no 36, it is an English text file, and the English audio is saved with "TEDTALK_PARTA_3_36_ENG.
10. Ensure you select the exact main folder i.e. public health or general domain folder, and that you store the English audio recorded in the allocated folder particular, paying attention to TED_TALK_MALARIA_ENGLISH_TRANSCRIPT, _PARTA, and _2 for the case of TED_TALK_MALARIA_ENGLISH_TRANSCRIPT_PARTA_2 as the name of a sub-folder.

11. Upload your recordings using this link Upload Audio Files while simultaneously submit the details of your recordings using the Google form provided on the bottom-right of this page.
12. Record a maximum of 2.5 hours and take a break every 30 minutes of recordings.
13. You can make use of the text areas on the right to store texts.

Submit the following details per audio recording using the bottom-right form:
1. File name you save the recording with.
2. The type of data you just recorded (either general or public health)
3. Duration (in seconds) of each of your recordings
4. Accent type utilised for the recording.

Press the "Record" button to start recording
Press the "Pause" button to pause and press again to resume
Press the "Stop" button to end the recording and download your file.
